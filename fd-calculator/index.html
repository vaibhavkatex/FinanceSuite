Fixed Deposit (FD) Guide

A Fixed Deposit (FD) is a secure financial instrument offered by banks and NBFCs in India where you deposit a lump sum amount for a fixed tenure at a predetermined rate of interest. The interest rate remains constant throughout the tenure, making it an extremely safe and predictable investment.

Compounding frequency refers to how often the interest is calculated and added to your principal. The more frequently interest compounds, the higher the final return. In India, most commercial banks compound interest quarterly by default, but some offer monthly or yearly schemes.

The final maturity value is computed using the compound interest formula:

A = P × (1 + r / (n × 100))(n × t)

Where:

  • A = Maturity Amount
  • P = Principal amount
  • r = Annual interest rate (percentage)
  • n = Compounding frequency per year (12 for monthly, 4 for quarterly, 2 for half-yearly, 1 for yearly)
  • t = Number of years
