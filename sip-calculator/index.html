Mutual Fund SIP Guide

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed amount of money regularly in mutual funds or equity schemes. It instills financial discipline, averages out stock market volatility (Rupee Cost Averaging), and harnesses the power of compounding to build long-term wealth.

A Step-up SIP (or Top-up SIP) allows you to automatically increase your monthly contribution by a fixed percentage (e.g., 10%) every year. As your income increases over time, stepping up your SIP allows you to build a significantly larger corpus in a shorter period compared to a flat SIP.

For a standard flat SIP, the future value is computed using the Future Value of Annuity formula:

FV = P × [((1 + i)n − 1) / i] × (1 + i)

Where:

  • FV = Future Value / Maturity Amount
  • P = Monthly investment amount
  • i = Monthly interest rate = (Annual interest rate / 12) / 100
  • n = Total number of months (years × 12)
