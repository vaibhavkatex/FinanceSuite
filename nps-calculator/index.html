National Pension System (NPS) Guide

The National Pension System (NPS) is a voluntary, long-term retirement savings scheme designed to provide social security to citizens in India. It is regulated by the Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority (PFRDA) and allows investments in mixtures of equity, corporate debt, and government bonds.

Upon reaching the age of 60, NPS rules mandate that you must use at least 40% of the accumulated corpus to purchase a life annuity plan from a registered Annuity Service Provider (which provides a regular monthly pension). The remaining 60% can be withdrawn as a tax-free lump sum.

NPS offers multiple tax benefits under the Income Tax Act, 1961:

  • Section 80CCD (1): Investments up to ₹1.5 Lakhs qualify for tax savings under the overall Section 80C limit.
  • Section 80CCD (1B): An additional deduction of up to ₹50,000 is available exclusively for NPS investments, over and above Section 80C.
