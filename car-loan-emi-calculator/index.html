Car Loan EMI Guide

Purchasing a car is an exciting milestone. Whether buying a new SUV or a certified pre-owned hatchback, banks and Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) in India offer car loans with tenures stretching up to 7 or 8 years. Interest rates are usually slightly higher than home loans and vary based on your CIBIL score.

Lenders in India prefer a CIBIL score of 750 and above for approving vehicle loans at the best interest rates. A lower score might attract higher rates or require a larger down payment.

Generally, new car loan interest rates are lower (ranging between 8.5% and 12% p.a.) compared to pre-owned/used car loans, which can range from 12% to 18% p.a. because of higher depreciation risk.
