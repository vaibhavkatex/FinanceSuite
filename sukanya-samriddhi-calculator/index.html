Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) Guidelines

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) is a savings scheme launched under the government's 'Beti Bachao Beti Padhao' campaign. Parents can open this account in the name of a girl child below the age of 10. The scheme offers one of the highest risk-free interest rates in India, coupled with EEE tax benefits.

In SSY, deposits must be made for the first 15 years from the date of opening the account. From Year 16 to Year 21, you do not need to make any deposits, but the balance in the account continues to compound annually at the current interest rate until the account matures after 21 years.

Withdrawals from the SSY account are permitted under the following conditions:

  • For Higher Education: Up to 50% of the balance at the end of the preceding financial year can be withdrawn when the girl turns 18 or passes Class 10.
  • On Marriage: The account can be closed early after the girl turns 18, subject to submission of marriage proof.
