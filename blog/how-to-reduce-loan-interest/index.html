How to Reduce Your Loan Interest Payable — Proven Strategies

Taking out a home loan or personal loan is a standard step towards achieving life goals. However, over a long tenure (like 20 or 30 years), the total interest you pay can easily exceed the principal amount you borrowed.

Here are the most effective, battle-tested strategies to reduce your total interest burden.

1. Make Regular Part-Prepayments

Prepaying even a small extra sum toward your principal loan balance can lead to compounding savings.

  • The Impact: Any extra payment goes 100% towards reducing your principal balance. Since monthly interest is calculated on the outstanding balance, lowering it saves interest for all subsequent months.
  • Pro Tip: Try the 1-EMI Prepayment Rule—prepay one extra EMI every year, which can shave 3 to 4 years off a 20-year home loan.

2. Increase Your EMI as Your Income Grows

As you get salary hikes or business profits, increase your monthly EMI amount.

  • Increasing your EMI by just 5% every year can reduce your overall tenure from 20 years to around 12 years, saving lakhs in interest.

3. Request a Home Loan Balance Transfer (HLBT)

If other banks are offering lower interest rates (especially if your CIBIL score has improved since you first took the loan), you can transfer your outstanding loan balance to the new bank.

  • Check the Math: Compare the transfer fees (processing fees, stamp duties) against the net interest savings. A difference of 0.50% or more is usually worth it.

4. Maintain a High CIBIL Score

A credit score of 750+ gives you leverage. You can negotiate with your current bank to lower your interest rate (switch from flat to floating, or demand a reduction inline with new customer offers).
