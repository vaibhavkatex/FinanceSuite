PPF Benefits & Tax Savings Explained

The Public Provident Fund (PPF) is one of the most reliable and trusted long-term savings schemes in India. Backed directly by the Central Government, it offers secure returns coupled with unmatched tax benefits.

Here is a complete breakdown of why you should include PPF in your investment portfolio.

1. Unmatched EEE Tax Status

The primary benefit that makes PPF stand out is its Exempt-Exempt-Exempt (EEE) tax classification:

  • Exempt 1 (Investment): Contributions up to ₹1.5 Lakhs in a financial year are fully deductible under Section 80C.
  • Exempt 2 (Interest): The interest earned annual on the PPF balance is completely exempt from income tax.
  • Exempt 3 (Maturity): The final maturity corpus after 15 years is 100% tax-free.

2. Guaranteed Government Safety

Unlike mutual funds or equity investments, PPF is not market-linked. The interest rate is reviewed and set by the Ministry of Finance every quarter. Since it is backed by the Government of India, there is zero credit risk.

3. Account Longevity and Flexibilities

  • Tenure: Standard tenure is 15 years.
  • Extensions: You can extend the account in blocks of 5 years indefinitely after maturity.
  • Minimum/Maximum Deposit: Invest as little as ₹500 or up to ₹1.5 Lakhs per financial year.

Summary Checklist for PPF Investors

  • Open account in any major bank or post office.
  • Try to deposit before the 5th of the month to maximize interest calculations.
  • Set up auto-debits for financial discipline.
