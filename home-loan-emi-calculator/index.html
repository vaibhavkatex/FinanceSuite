Understanding Home Loan EMIs

A Home Loan is one of the largest financial commitments an individual makes. Calculating your Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) helps you plan your monthly budget and check your affordability before applying to major Indian banks like SBI, HDFC, or ICICI.

Home loan EMIs in India are computed using the reducing balance method. The math formula is:

EMI = [P × r × (1 + r)n] / [(1 + r)n − 1]

Where:

  • P = Principal Loan Amount
  • r = Monthly Interest Rate (annual interest rate divided by 12, then divided by 100)
  • n = Loan Tenure in Months (years multiplied by 12)

When you prepay a portion of your home loan, that extra amount is directly subtracted from your outstanding principal balance. Since interest is calculated monthly on the remaining balance, prepaying reduces your principal faster, leading to a shorter tenure and substantial savings in total interest payable.

In a reducing balance method, interest is calculated on the outstanding loan amount at the end of each month. As you pay your EMI each month, a portion goes towards principal repayment, which reduces the outstanding loan balance. Therefore, the interest for the next month is computed on this lower balance, and the proportion of your EMI going towards principal increases over time.
